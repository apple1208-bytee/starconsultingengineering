### Managing Thermal Stratification

In nuclear primary and secondary coolant loops, fluids of vastly different temperatures often mix during startup, shutdown, or transient events, leading to severe thermal stratification. This complex phenomenon causes highly uneven thermal expansion across the cross-section of the pipe, inducing severe bowing, warping, and unprecedented low-cycle fatigue stresses that can quickly crack thick-walled stainless steel. Star Consulting utilizes advanced computational fluid dynamics (CFD) combined closely with finite element analysis (FEA) to accurately model these complex thermal profiles. We design highly specialized support schemes and routing configurations that specifically mitigate the risk of thermal fatigue failure in critical surge and injection lines.

Nuclear piping must be mathematically proven to survive "Design Basis Accidents" (DBA).
