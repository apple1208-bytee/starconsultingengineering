## Navigating Sector-Specific Challenges

The Oil & Gas sector presents a confluence of hostile operating conditions that push piping materials and support structures to their absolute limits. We engineer systems specifically to withstand these punishing realities.

![Oil & gas facility photo](https://images.pexels.com/photos/3192662/pexels-photo-3192662.jpeg)

**High-Pressure Sour Service:** Hydrogen Sulfide (H₂S) is a deadly, highly corrosive byproduct found in many crude and natural gas streams. It causes Sulfide Stress Cracking (SSC) in susceptible metals. We navigate the rigid material selection constraints of NACE MR0175/ISO 15156, ensuring that the piping metallurgy and weld hardness levels are inherently resistant to sour service environments.

**Subsea Tie-Ins and Offshore Challenges:** Offshore platforms operate in corrosive salt-spray environments and must withstand massive environmental loads (wave, wind, current) in addition to operational stress. Space is highly constrained. We design ultra-compact piping module skids that accommodate necessary thermal expansion while remaining perfectly rigid against environmental excitation.

**Aging Infrastructure:** The global O&G infrastructure is aging. Plant managers are constantly battling corrosion under insulation (CUI) and fatigue cracking. Our [Troubleshooting](/services/engineering-troubleshooting) teams deploy rapidly to assess in-service failures, calculate the remaining life of degraded piping, and engineer safe fitness-for-service (FFS) modifications that extend operational viability without shutting down production.

**Flare Systems and HIPPS Integration:** Emergency depressurization systems are the final line of defense. High-Integrity Pressure Protection Systems (HIPPS) close rapidly to isolate high pressure, creating massive transient shockwaves. Meanwhile, flare headers must be engineered to withstand sudden, violent surges of mixed-phase hydrocarbons and extreme thermal shock. We model these highly dynamic events to ensure relief systems never fail when called upon. For an example of our approach to transient events, read our [pressure surge case study](/blog/pressure-surge-case-study).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do you handle piping stress in extreme high-temperature refinery units?**
For systems exceeding 800°F, such as FCC units or delayed cokers, we must account for material creep - the slow, permanent deformation of steel under constant stress at high temperatures. We execute advanced time-history and creep-fatigue interaction analyses, selecting specialized alloys (like P91 or 347H stainless) and designing supports that accommodate massive, multi-directional thermal expansion without binding.

**What is the biggest risk with reciprocating compressors in gas processing?**
Pulsation-induced acoustic resonance. The compressor generates pressure waves that travel through the gas. If the frequency of these waves matches the mechanical natural frequency of the piping system, extreme shaking occurs. We perform API 618 Design Approach 3 studies to design pulsation dampeners and strategically place dynamic restraints to alter the system's mechanical frequency, guaranteeing a safe margin of separation.

**Can you assess aging offshore piping without shutting down production?**
Yes. Using advanced ultrasonic testing (UT) and strain gauging, we measure the actual wall thickness and live operational stress of the pipe while it is in service. We then apply API 570 Fitness-For-Service (FFS) calculations to determine if the line is safe to operate, requires a temporary clamp, or necessitates a planned shutdown for replacement.
