# Technical Audits

Technical piping audits are rigorous, highly specialized engineering investigations designed to assess the safety, integrity, and regulatory compliance of both new designs and aging industrial infrastructure. Unlike routine maintenance checks, a comprehensive technical piping audit involves a deep forensic dive into mechanical design parameters, metallurgical degradation, and operational history. We conduct meticulous fitness for service evaluations, complex integrity assessments, and exhaustive code compliance reviews to identify hidden vulnerabilities before they manifest as catastrophic failures. Whether evaluating a legacy refinery suffering from severe wall-thinning corrosion, auditing a newly proposed EPC design for critical flaws, or investigating the root cause of a sudden pipeline rupture, our technical audits provide facility owners with the definitive, data-backed engineering intelligence required to make critical repair, replacement, or continued-operation decisions safely.

## Audit Types

Industrial facilities require different analytical approaches depending on their lifecycle stage and operational challenges. We offer a comprehensive suite of highly specialized technical audits tailored to specific engineering needs.

*   **Design Audit (Third-Party Review):** Conducted before physical construction begins. We act as an independent authority to meticulously review EPC contractor calculations, 3D models, and stress reports, ensuring the proposed design is mathematically sound, clash-free, and strictly code-compliant.
*   **In-Service Integrity Audit:** Focused on operating plants. We review historical inspection data (NDT, ultrasonic thickness readings), analyze corrosion rates, and assess the remaining operational life of the piping network against its original design baseline.
*   **Fitness-for-Service (FFS) Assessment:** The most complex audit, executed per API 579 methodologies. When severe flaws (crack-like defects, localized extreme corrosion, or fire damage) are discovered, we utilize advanced Finite Element Analysis (FEA) to definitively determine if the damaged pipe can safely remain in service under modified operating parameters.
*   **Code Compliance Gap Analysis:** We audit older, "grandfathered" facilities against modern, significantly stricter engineering codes (like updated revisions of ASME B31.3) to identify safety gaps and formulate compliance upgrade roadmaps.
*   **Support Condition Audit:** A targeted physical and analytical inspection of the pipe support infrastructure. We identify "bottomed-out" spring hangers, sheared guides, and heavily corroded baseplates that drastically alter the intended stress profile of the piping system.

## Audit Process

A technical audit is a highly structured forensic investigation. We follow a rigorous, multi-staged process to ensure absolute thoroughness and accuracy in our findings.

The audit process initiates with an exhaustive **Document Review**. Our engineers meticulously analyze original P&IDs, isometric drawings, historical stress reports, material test certificates (MTRs), and years of NDT inspection logs to establish the baseline truth of the system. Following this, we conduct a comprehensive **Field Inspection**. Utilizing laser scanning and visual assessment, we verify that the "as-built" physical reality of the plant actually matches the theoretical engineering drawings, specifically hunting for un-engineered modifications or severe physical degradation. 

Armed with field data and documentation, we proceed to the **Analysis** phase. We rebuild or update mathematical stress models to reflect the *actual* current condition of the pipe (incorporating measured corrosion loss and shifted supports) and re-run the operational load cases. The culmination of this process is the **Findings Report**, which details every overstressed node and structural vulnerability. Finally, we provide actionable **Recommendations**, detailing the exact engineered repairs, support modifications, or pressure de-ratings required to restore the system to full code compliance and operational safety.

## Standards

Our technical audits are strictly governed by the supreme legal and technical standards of the mechanical engineering industry. We base every finding, calculation, and recommendation on these unyielding codes.

For evaluating physical degradation and severe flaws, we strictly utilize **API 579-1/ASME FFS-1 (Fitness-For-Service)**. This incredibly complex standard provides the mathematical fracture mechanics and assessment procedures required to safely evaluate crack-like flaws, local thin areas (LTAs), and severe pitting corrosion to prevent catastrophic rupture.

For ongoing operational integrity and inspection scheduling, we adhere to **API 570 (Piping Inspection Code: In-service Inspection, Rating, Repair, and Alteration of Piping Systems)**. This standard dictates how we calculate remaining life and establish safe re-inspection intervals. We supplement this with **API 574**, which guides our physical inspection practices. Finally, the ultimate baseline for all stress and pressure calculations remains **ASME B31.3 (Process Piping)** or its equivalent sister codes, ensuring that even degraded systems maintain the fundamental safety factors required by law.

## Deliverables

We do not simply hand over raw data; we deliver clear, authoritative engineering intelligence designed for executive decision-making and immediate field implementation.

*   **Executive Summary:** A high-level, clear-language brief designed for plant managers and stakeholders, immediately outlining critical safety risks, regulatory compliance status, and the urgent actions required.
*   **Detailed Findings:** An exhaustive technical appendix for mechanical engineers, containing all updated CAESAR II stress outputs, FEA color plots, metallurgical assessments, and detailed photographic evidence of field anomalies.
*   **Risk-Ranked Recommendations:** We do not treat all issues equally. We provide a heavily prioritized remediation roadmap, categorizing required repairs by severity (Immediate Shutdown, Next Turnaround, Long-Term Monitoring) based strictly on calculated failure probabilities.
*   **Re-Inspection Schedule:** A mathematically derived schedule based on calculated corrosion rates and API 570 guidelines, telling your reliability team exactly when and where to inspect the system next to maintain continuous safety.

## When to Audit

Technical audits are critical interventions required at specific, high-risk junctions in an industrial facility's lifecycle. 

They are absolutely essential during a **Pre-Acquisition** due diligence phase, allowing buyers to identify hidden multi-million dollar deferred maintenance liabilities before finalizing a purchase. They are mandatory **Post-Incident**, where a forensic audit must determine the exact root cause of a leak, rupture, or fire to prevent recurrence and satisfy insurance and regulatory bodies. 

Audits are also frequently triggered by a **Regulatory Requirement**, ensuring compliance with updated OSHA or local environmental laws. Finally, an audit is critical before any **Change of Service**. If a pipeline originally designed for low-pressure water is being repurposed for high-pressure hazardous chemicals, a complete re-baseline audit is legally and technically mandatory to guarantee the legacy steel can safely handle the new, severe operating envelope.
